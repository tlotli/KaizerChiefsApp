    Tshabalala shoulders blame for Chiefs draw

    Chiefs gave up their lead twice during the encounter where Aces snatched a late equalizer at the Mbombela Stadium

    Tshabalala, who won the man-of-the-match award, was left desolated after failing to steer Chiefs to their first win in six games. “I think it was a great game of football. We showed hunger; we were very determined from the first minute and we created a lot of chances,” Tshabalala told SuperSport. “I’ve left the team down in the last minute, and we got caught open in the midfield, and they got the equaliser. But overall I think we did well and I owe it to the team. “There is chemistry in the team; there is unity in the team. With the game we played, we cannot blame the coach. “Us players took the responsibility that when we lose or draw, we all take the stick, not the coach. “We have to protect the coach by bringing in positive results, and I think we should have won tonight. We were a bit unlucky with the draw.
