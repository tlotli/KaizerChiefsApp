    Twala: We’ll bounce back

    The league champions find themselves fourth on the table with 40 points from 24 matches and 15 points shy of leaders Mamelodi Sundowns following a string of poor results throughout the campaign.

    The former Orlando Pirates winger believes Amakhosi can end their seven-game winless run, when they face the Urban Warriors, who are sitting nineth in the league standings on Saturday. ” We are working hard at training and soon the wanted results will come,” Twala told his club’s official website. “Ajax are a good side,” added Twala. ” We need the points as much as they do. It will be a tough match.” Twala joined the Soweto-based club from Chippa United during the January transfer window. His impressive displays recently earned him a national team call-up for the Africa Cup of Nations qualifiers against Cameroon. “It was a great motivator to be selected for the national team.
